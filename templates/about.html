About

E2B was created by three highschool students, Ari Peres, Joe Yan, and Aureliano Applebee. The website's primary goal is to take on a task of social justice and to help the world. In today's world, many blind people need to have easy access to reading. Although many english to braille translators already exist they only go half the mile. Blind people can't run their hands across a flat screen to read translated english to braille, so E2B aims to create an easily printed 3D model of braille. Although 3D printers can be very expensive its easy to get access to local printers for very cheap. Although there are also specific machines made for braille they are very expensive and inaccessible. E2B takes English text, converts it to Braille, and then creates a 3D model of the Braille. For further accessibility, E2B offers many modifations to the 3D model.
